I don’t think the current system provides enough accountability for
short-term rentals in San Francisco. It depends too much on hosts to
report accurate information and on others to notice and report issues.
This makes enforcement reactive instead of proactive, which allows
noncompliance to slip through. A better approach would be for Airbnb to
verify policy numbers directly with the city before allowing listings to
go live, which would reduce reliance on complaints and improve
accountability.

One important factor in the legality of web scraping is whether the data
is truly public and how it is accessed. There is a clear difference
between collecting information from openly accessible webpages and
bypassing restrictions like logins or security measures. This matters
because scraping should not involve violating privacy or platform
protections, even if the data seems available.

Two important guidelines for web scraping are having a clear purpose and
minimizing harm. Scraping should be done for legitimate reasons like
research or accountability, not for exploiting users. It should also
limit the amount of data collected and avoid exposing sensitive personal
information. Even public data should be handled carefully to avoid
negative consequences.
