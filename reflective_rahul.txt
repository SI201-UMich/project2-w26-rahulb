One important factor when thinking about the legality of web scraping is whether the data is actually public and how it is accessed. There’s a difference between just taking information from pages anyone can see and trying to get around things that are deliberately designed to block access, like logins or blockers. Even if something is online, it doesn’t mean it’s fair to ignore those barriers and take the data anyway. 

Two guidelines for web scraping could be having a clear purpose and being thoughtful about how the data is shared. It’s important to have a real reason for scraping, like research or accountability, rather than just collecting data for no reason or to exploit users. It’s also important to limit how much data you collect and avoid exposing sensitive information. Even if data is public, it should still be handled carefully to avoid negative consequences.
