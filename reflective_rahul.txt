One important factor when thinking about the legality of web scraping is whether the data is truly public and how to access it. There’s a difference between simply taking information from pages that everyone can see and trying to get around things that are deliberately designed to block access, like logins or blockers. Even if some of them are online, it doesn’t mean it’s fair to ignore those barriers and get the data anyway. 

Two guidelines for web scraping are to have clear goals and be thoughtful about how the data is shared. It is important to have a real reason to think, such as research or accountability, rather than just collecting data for no reason or to exploit users. It’s also important to limit how much data you collect and avoid exposing sensitive information. Even if the data is public, it still has to be handled carefully to avoid any negative consequences.
